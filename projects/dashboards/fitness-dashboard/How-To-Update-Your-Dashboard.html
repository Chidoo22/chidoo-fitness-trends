How To Keep Your
Fitness Dashboard Fresh

A simple guide to updating your dashboard with new health data

Made for Chido 💖

Recommended: update every 2 weeks or once a month

💡 What is this guide about?

Your fitness dashboard shows your health data (steps, heart rate, calories, workouts, weight). But that data gets old! This guide shows you how to refresh it with new data from your iPhone. It takes about 2 minutes and you only need to do it every 2-4 weeks.

A Export Data from Your iPhone
1

📱 Open the Health App

Find the Health app on your iPhone. It has a white icon with a red/pink heart. Tap it to open.

2

👤 Tap Your Profile Picture

In the top-right corner of the Health app, you'll see your profile picture (or your initials in a circle). Tap on it.

3

📥 Tap "Export All Health Data"

Scroll down on the profile page. Near the bottom, you'll see a button that says "Export All Health Data". Tap it.

💡 A popup will ask "Are you sure?" — Tap "Export" to confirm.
4

⏳ Wait for the Export

Your iPhone will now prepare all your health data. This can take 1-5 minutes depending on how much data you have. You'll see a progress spinner. Just wait — don't close the app.

⚠️ The file can be large (100MB-500MB+). Make sure you have storage space on your phone.
5

📤 Send the File to Your Computer

When the export is done, a share sheet appears. You have several options:

Option A (Easiest): Tap "Save to Files" → choose iCloud Drive. Then open iCloud Drive on your Mac/PC to find it.

Option B: Tap AirDrop and send it directly to your Mac (if nearby).

Option C: Email it to yourself (only if file is small enough).

📁 The file is called "export.zip". Save it somewhere you can find it later (like Desktop or Downloads).
6

📂 Unzip the File (Optional)

On your computer, double-click the export.zip file to unzip it. Inside you'll find a file called export.xml — that's the important one. But you can also upload the ZIP directly to the dashboard — it will extract it for you.

B Upload to Your Dashboard
7

🌐 Open Your Fitness Dashboard

Open the fitness_dashboard.html file in your web browser (Safari, Chrome, etc.). Just double-click the file on your computer.

8

📤 Click the "Import Data" Button

In the dashboard, look for the "Import Data" button — it's in the top area near the filters, or in the nav bar. Click it.

9

📁 Select Your Export File

A file picker will open. Navigate to where you saved the file and select either:

• export.zip (the ZIP file directly), OR

• export.xml (the unzipped XML file)

Click "Open" or "Choose".

10

⏳ Wait for Processing

The dashboard will read and process your health data. This may take 10-60 seconds depending on file size. You'll see a progress indicator.

✨ Done! All your charts, KPIs, badges, and predictions will update with your latest data. The dashboard saves this data locally, so next time you open it, your data is still there.

📊 What Data Gets Imported?

  • ✓ Steps — Daily step count from iPhone + Apple Watch
  • ✓ Heart Rate — Resting HR from Apple Watch or Oura Ring
  • ✓ Calories — Active energy burned daily
  • ✓ Workouts — Type, duration, and date of each session
  • ✓ Weight — Body weight (if you log it or have a smart scale)
  • ✓ VO2 Max — Cardiorespiratory fitness estimates
  • ✓ HRV — Heart rate variability (if tracked by Apple Watch)
? Common Questions
Is my health data safe?
Yes! Your data never leaves your computer. The dashboard processes everything locally in your browser. Nothing is sent to any server or cloud service.
Will I lose my old data when I import new data?
No. Each import includes ALL your Apple Health history. So new imports automatically include old + new data combined.
What if the file is too large?
If you have years of data, the XML file can be 500MB+. The dashboard handles this by processing it in chunks. It may take a minute, but it will work. If it's very slow, try using Chrome instead of Safari.
Can I use this on my phone?
The dashboard works on phone browsers, but importing large XML files works better on a computer. You can export on your phone and upload on your computer.
What if I don't have an Apple Watch?
That's fine! Your iPhone alone tracks steps, flights climbed, and distance. Heart rate and HRV require a wearable (Apple Watch, Oura Ring, etc.), but the dashboard will simply show "No data" for those metrics.

📋 Quick Reference (Stick This on Your Fridge!)

Every 2 weeks:
1. iPhone → Health app → Profile → Export All Health Data
2. Save export.zip to computer (AirDrop or iCloud)
3. Open fitness_dashboard.html → Import Data → Select file
4. Wait 30 seconds → Done! ✨
